I am an academic researcher at GT, specializing in atmospheric chemistry and air quality modeling. My research primarily focuses on understanding air pollutants measurements, including VOCs and trace gases, to better characterize atmospheric chemical processes.

Currently, my work involves advanced environmental data analysis and visualization using Python and MATLAB. I extensively utilize models and tools such as the Framework for 0-D Atmospheric Modeling (F0AM) and EPA PMF 5.0 for source apportionment and chemical transport simulations. 

I am passionate about developing robust methodologies to process complex meteorological variables and instrument data (such as PTR-MS and GC CIMS data), ultimately contributing to a clearer understanding of our atmospheric environment.
